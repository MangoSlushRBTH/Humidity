Relative humidity and indoor health risk

The 35–50% RH zone minimises mold growth, viral transmission, and asthma risk simultaneously.

Mold / fungi growth Viral transmission Asthma / allergic rhinitis
Mold risk rises sharply above 60% RH. Viral transmission peaks below 30% and above 70%. Asthma risk is elevated at both extremes. The optimal zone is 35–50% RH.

Curves are schematic representations based on Sterling (1985), Lowen et al. (2007), Shaman & Kaplan (2009), and ASHRAE Standard 55. Relative risk normalised to 0–100 for cross-category comparability. Recommended zone (35–50% RH) reflects Southeast U.S. climate conditions; 40–55% RH is the broader WHO/ASHRAE consensus for temperate climates.
